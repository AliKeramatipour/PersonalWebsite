About Me

Hello! I'm Ali.

I'm currently an undergraduate Computer Engineering student at the University of Tehran. I also am a research assistant working under the supervision of Dr. Bahrak and Dr. Shariatpanahi.

The research area I am interested in is mainly Theoretical CS. During recent years, I have worked on a few open problems mainly related to Graph Theory. My work resulted in publishing a paper titled “Zombie number of the Cartesian product of graphs” in Discrete Applied Mathematics journal. I am currently working on the “Maximum Multicast Throughput” problem under the supervision of Dr. Seyed Pooya Shariatpanahi.

Research Experience and Publications

  • Zombie number of the Cartesian product of graphs, Ali Keramatipour, Behnam Bahrak

    Studied Zombies and Survivor game and its variants. Proved the Zombie number problem belongs to NP-Hard, also proved the correctness of conjectures about Zombie number of the Cartesian product of graphs.

  • Maximum Multicast Throughput, Ali Keramatipour, Seyed Pooya Shariatpanahi, Behnam Bahrak

    For a network using the coded caching scheme, what would be its maximum throughput?
    Proved the maximum multicast throughput problem belongs to NP-Hard, also developed an approximation algorithm. Currently trying to improve the constant approximation factor or prove no better PTIME algorithm exists. (Paper under preparation, will be provided upon request)
